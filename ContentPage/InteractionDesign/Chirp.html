Chirp -- Companion Robot for Young Piano Learner
Intelligent Robot, Interaction Design
School Project
2018.3 - 2018.6 (Onging)
Chirp is a robot for young piano learner at the age of 5 - 12. Chinese children at this age are often pushed to learn piano by their parents but hard to grow interests in piano by themselves. That makes their learning process pretty painful.
That's why we make chirp. It has a cute bird-like body and its language is music. It feeds on piano notes, so when children is practicing, Chirp is being feed. By this way, we can turn the painful practicing into a simulation game.
And of course, Chirp can play music games with kids. We designed some games to help kids learn by playing.
This is a project we did during the 2018 Interaction Design course. My main role in this project is designing interaction pattern for Chirp and controlling the servo for its head to move.
This project got first prize in China-US Young Maker Competition for Shanghai. We are still building on it and will go to Beijing in 2018.8 for the final competition.
