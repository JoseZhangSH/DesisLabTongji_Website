Cityrama
Panorama, Experiment Film
School Project
2017.5
My inspiration came from an historical film technology — Cinerama. It is a widescreen process that originally projected images simultaneously from three synchronized 35 mm projectors onto a huge, deeply curved screen. It gives people a wider and more familiar perspective than normal 35mm films.

I really love this idea and want to shot a three-camera panorama film. So I chose the most affordable camera — YI Action Camera as the starting point. I made a base with laser-cutting machine, sticked three YI Action Camera on it, and connected the camera in the middle to a tripod. Then I just took this device and shot everywhere in the city.

Because each camera has a fisheye lens, the final stitched images’ field of view is more than 180 degrees. And the objects in the scene are distorting in different levels. I tried to shoot at different types of spaces, not only open spaces that Cinerama was designed to show, but also some narrow spaces to find a unique way of using this interesting perspective and distortion.

Through the shooting, I find that the movements of people, cars and shadows in the city are really interesting. The image differs when I’m moving, or the city is moving. The moving patterns show a breath-like rhythm of the city life . I tried to capture this feeling and tweak with speed, composition and audio to emphasize it. Through this film, I’m paying tribute to Cinerama.
